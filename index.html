Tribute to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

"The Missile Man of India" & Former President

About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear programs, earning him the title of *“Missile Man of India.”*

Born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering. He worked as a scientist and administrator at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

Dr. Kalam inspired millions of students across the country through his books, speeches, and dedication to education. His vision for India’s growth was captured in his famous book, *“India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium.”*

“Dream, Dream, Dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”

Legacy

Even after his presidency, Dr. Kalam remained dedicated to teaching and mentoring students. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management, Shillong. His life continues to inspire generations to come.
